# January 2026 - Asia/Oceania Summary

## Overview

Early January 2026 saw significant cybersecurity incidents in the Asia-Pacific region, with New Zealand experiencing a major healthcare data breach and Israeli security personnel being targeted by Iranian state-sponsored actors. The Manage My Health patient portal breach potentially affected up to 126,000 New Zealanders, while Israeli defense and security workers faced sophisticated spear-phishing campaigns from APT42 (Charming Kitten).

## Key Trends

### Healthcare Data Breach in New Zealand

New Zealand's private patient portal Manage My Health suffered unauthorized access to health documents affecting approximately 6-7% of its 1.8 million users (~126,000 people). The breach, reported on December 30, 2025, highlights vulnerabilities in digital health infrastructure and the attractiveness of medical records to cybercriminals.

### Iranian APT Targeting Israel

Iran-linked APT42 (also known as Charming Kitten) leveraged infrastructure on January 2 to conduct spear-phishing attacks against Israeli security and defense workers via WhatsApp. The campaign used social engineering and impersonation tactics to steal credentials and sensitive information.

### Mobile Messaging Platforms as Attack Vectors

The use of WhatsApp for spear-phishing in the APT42 campaign demonstrates that threat actors are increasingly leveraging mobile messaging platforms rather than traditional email to bypass security controls and reach high-value targets.

- **Date:** 2025-12-30
- **Target:** Manage My Health (New Zealand patient portal)
- **Attack Type:** Unauthorized access / Data breach
- **Impact:** ~126,000 patients affected (~6-7% of 1.8M users); private health documents accessed

A widely used patient portal in New Zealand reported a cyber breach two days before the New Year, affecting up to 126,000 people. Manage My Health has identified general practices whose patients have had their private health information breached in the cybersecurity hack. The portal serves approximately 1.8 million users across New Zealand.

### January 2, 2026 – APT42 Spear-Phishing Campaign Against Israel

- **Date:** 2026-01-02
- **Target:** Israeli security and defense workers
- **Attack Type:** Spear-phishing via WhatsApp
- **Impact:** Credential theft attempts; information stealing
- **Attribution:** APT42 (Charming Kitten) - Iranian state-backed
- **Status:** Ongoing campaign; alerts issued
- **Source:** https://www.scworld.com/brief/apt42-linked-infrastructure-leveraged-in-israel-targeted-spear-phishing-attack

Israeli security and defense workers were targeted by an ongoing spear-phishing campaign involving infrastructure linked to Iranian state-backed threat operation APT42, also known as Charming Kitten. Threat actors used WhatsApp to send malicious messages purporting to be from legitimate organizations, luring recipients into clicking shortened URLs that redirect to credential- and information-stealing websites. The attack also involved the distribution of illicit files for an expanded attack surface.

## Threat Actor Activity

### APT42 (Charming Kitten)

Iranian state-backed threat operation APT42, also known as Charming Kitten, conducted a sophisticated spear-phishing campaign on January 2 targeting Israeli security and defense workers. The group used WhatsApp to deliver malicious messages impersonating legitimate organizations. They employed shortened URLs to redirect victims to credential-stealing websites and distributed illicit files to expand their attack surface. Israel's National Cyber Directorate issued alerts warning organizations to watch for known APT42 indicators and reinforce anti-phishing training, especially for WhatsApp communications.

### Unknown Threat Actor (Manage My Health)

An unidentified threat actor conducted unauthorized access to the Manage My Health patient portal in New Zealand. The attacker accessed private health documents of approximately 126,000 patients. The breach demonstrates sophisticated targeting of healthcare infrastructure in the region.

## Legislation & Policy Updates

### Israel National Cyber Directorate Alert

Following the APT42 spear-phishing campaign, Israel's National Cyber Directorate issued an official alert on January 2, 2026, warning security and defense organizations about the ongoing threat. The alert included known indicators of compromise (IOCs) associated with APT42 infrastructure and recommended enhanced anti-phishing training, particularly for communications via WhatsApp and other mobile messaging platforms.

### New Zealand Privacy Response

Following the Manage My Health breach, New Zealand privacy authorities and health officials have been working with affected GP practices to identify and notify impacted patients. The incident has prompted discussions about strengthening security requirements for digital health platforms handling sensitive medical information.

## Geopolitical Cyber Threats

### Iran-Israel Cyber Tensions

The APT42 campaign highlights the ongoing cyber tensions between Iran and Israel. Iranian state-backed groups continue to target Israeli defense, security, and technology sectors using sophisticated social engineering techniques. The use of WhatsApp as an attack vector suggests adversaries are adapting to security controls that may be more robust for traditional email-based attacks.

### Regional Security Implications

The targeting of security professionals in Israel has broader implications for regional cybersecurity. Defense organizations across the Asia-Pacific region should be aware that state-sponsored actors are actively targeting personnel through mobile platforms and social engineering, not just traditional network intrusions.

## Conclusion

The first days of January 2026 in the Asia-Pacific region demonstrated both opportunistic cybercrime (New Zealand healthcare breach) and targeted state-sponsored espionage (APT42 campaign against Israel). Healthcare infrastructure remains vulnerable to unauthorized access, while defense and security personnel face sophisticated spear-phishing campaigns via mobile platforms. Organizations in the region must strengthen both technical controls and human factors (security awareness) to defend against these evolving threats.
