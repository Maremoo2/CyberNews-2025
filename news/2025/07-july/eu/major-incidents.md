# July 2025 - EU Major Incidents

## Major Data Breaches in the EU

### July 12, 2025 – France Travail

- **Date:** July 12, 2025
- **Target:** France Travail (France's national employment agency)
- **Attack Type:** Data breach via third-party system
- **Impact:** Personal details of ~340,000 job seekers exposed
- **Attribution:** Not explicitly attributed; underscores third-party risk
- **Details:** Attackers compromised a training partner's platform (Kairos) using infostealer malware to bypass 2FA and access France Travail's jobseeker database. Personal details of approximately 340,000 job seekers (names, addresses, phone numbers, national IDs) were exposed. The breach underscores the risks posed by third-party vendors and the need for stronger session management and device security. Combining 2FA with device monitoring and behavioral analysis would help prevent similar breaches.
- **Source:** [DataBreaches.net](https://databreaches.net), [Eye World](https://eye.world), [Strobes](https://strobes.co)

### July 16, 2025 – Louis Vuitton

- **Date:** July 16, 2025
- **Target:** Louis Vuitton (Luxury retailer, HQ in France)
- **Attack Type:** Customer data breaches across EU regions
- **Impact:** Customer personal information exposed across multiple countries
- **Attribution:** Believed to be the work of ShinyHunters extortion group
- **Details:** The company revealed that customer databases in multiple countries (including the UK, Italy, Sweden, and Turkey) were compromised by a single cyberattack. Exposed personal information of clients included names, contact info, passport numbers, addresses and purchase history, though no payment card data was taken. Company statements linked the regional breaches to one attack, likely by ShinyHunters.
- **Source:** [CM Alliance - July 2025 Cyber Attacks](https://cm-alliance.com)

### July 22, 2025 – Affidea

- **Date:** July 22, 2025
- **Target:** Affidea (Pan-European healthcare network)
- **Attack Type:** Major health provider breach
- **Impact:** Disruption of medical services and operations across multiple EU nations
- **Attribution:** Unknown threat actor
- **Details:** Affidea, which operates diagnostic clinics in multiple EU countries, disclosed a cyberattack that forced it to take clinical IT systems offline across several nations. Disruption of medical services and operations (radiology/MRI systems, etc.) in multiple EU member states occurred, with patient care delays, though no specific patient data leakage was confirmed at the time. The incident highlighted vulnerabilities in healthcare networks spanning borders.
- **Source:** [CM Alliance - July 2025 Cyber Attacks](https://cm-alliance.com)

### July 27, 2025 – Naval Group

- **Date:** July 27, 2025
- **Target:** Naval Group (France's warship builder)
- **Attack Type:** Defense industry data leak
- **Impact:** Approximately 1 TB of internal data leaked
- **Attribution:** Unknown (perpetrator's identity not confirmed)
- **Details:** A hacker publicly leaked approximately 1 TB of internal data from Naval Group, which designs submarines and warships. The dump allegedly included sensitive documents on submarine and ship designs, project plans, and supplier details, posing potential national security risks. French authorities opened an investigation, as this breach underscored the threat to defense contractors.
- **Source:** [CM Alliance - July 2025 Cyber Attacks](https://cm-alliance.com), BleepingComputer

### July 28, 2025 – Orange S.A.

- **Date:** July 28, 2025
- **Target:** Orange S.A. (France's telecom giant)
- **Attack Type:** Supply-chain email platform breach
- **Impact:** Undisclosed number of European customers had data exposed
- **Attribution:** Not publicly identified; under investigation
- **Details:** Orange disclosed that a cyberattack targeted one of its third-party email service providers, resulting in unauthorized access to customers' personal data. Undisclosed number of European customers had data like email addresses and possibly other contact details exposed via the compromised email platform. Orange had to notify regulators and users, though core telecom systems weren't affected.
- **Source:** [CM Alliance - July 2025 Cyber Attacks](https://cm-alliance.com), BleepingComputer

## Notable Cyber Attacks on EU Organizations

### July 1, 2025 – Welthungerhilfe (Germany)

- **Date:** July 1, 2025
- **Target:** Welthungerhilfe (Major German hunger-relief charity)
- **Attack Type:** Ransomware attack on NGO
- **Impact:** Charity operations disrupted; donor and organizational information at risk
- **Attribution:** Rhysida ransomware group (RaaS) claimed responsibility
- **Details:** Rhysida ransomware actors attacked Welthungerhilfe, a major German hunger-relief charity. The gang stole and encrypted donor and internal data, demanding approximately 20 BTC (~$2.1 million) for decryption. Welthungerhilfe refused to pay and shut down affected systems, working with investigators to respond.
- **Source:** [CM Alliance - July 2025 Cyber Attacks](https://cm-alliance.com), The Record

### July 1, 2025 – International Criminal Court (ICC)

- **Date:** July 1, 2025
- **Target:** International Criminal Court (The Hague)
- **Attack Type:** Sophisticated network breach
- **Impact:** Some court systems taken offline; investigation launched
- **Attribution:** Unknown (described as "sophisticated")
- **Details:** The ICC in the Netherlands suffered a targeted cyberattack that disrupted its IT systems. Some court systems were taken offline as a precaution, and an investigation was launched. The ICC did not confirm data theft publicly, and by month's end no evidence of data leaks had emerged. The incident raised concerns about threat actors possibly seeking sensitive info on war crimes cases.
- **Source:** [CM Alliance - July 2025 Cyber Attacks](https://cm-alliance.com), BleepingComputer

### July 3, 2025 – Telefónica S.A. (Spain)

- **Date:** July 3, 2025
- **Target:** Telefónica S.A. (Spanish telecom giant)
- **Attack Type:** Corporate data leak by hacker
- **Impact:** 2.6 GB sample data leaked (part of alleged 106 GB haul)
- **Attribution:** Hacker "Rey" associated with HellCat ransomware group
- **Details:** A hacker known as "Rey," associated with the HellCat ransomware group, leaked a 2.6 GB sample of data allegedly stolen from telecom giant Telefónica. The dump (part of an alleged 106 GB haul) contained over 20,000 internal files – including emails, invoices, and customer/employee data – from a May 30 breach of Telefónica's network. Exposure of confidential internal communications and personal data occurred; Telefónica was investigating whether this was a new breach or related to a past incident.
- **Source:** [CM Alliance - July 2025 Cyber Attacks](https://cm-alliance.com), BleepingComputer

### Late July 2025 – European Airports Third-Party Ransomware

- **Date:** Late July 2025
- **Target:** Collins Aerospace's MUSE check-in/boarding system
- **Attack Type:** Third-party contractor ransomware
- **Impact:** Flight disruptions at multiple European airports (Heathrow, Brussels, Berlin)
- **Attribution:** Not publicly identified by July; investigation continued
- **Details:** A noteworthy incident caused flight disruptions at multiple European airports when a third-party contractor was hit by ransomware. Collins Aerospace's MUSE check-in/boarding system went down over a weekend, snarling operations at major hubs like Heathrow (UK), Brussels, and Berlin. ENISA confirmed ransomware as the cause and law enforcement opened probes. This incident illustrated the ripple effect of supply-chain attacks on EU transportation.
- **Source:** [Industrial Cyber](https://industrialcyber.co), ENISA
