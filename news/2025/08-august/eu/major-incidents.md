# August 2025 - EU Major Incidents

## Major Data Breaches

### Aug 4, 2025 – Chanel (France)

- **Date:** August 4, 2025
- **Target:** Chanel (Luxury fashion house, France)
- **Attack Type:** Data Breach via third-party Salesforce CRM compromise
- **Impact:** U.S. customer contact database accessed (names, emails, phone numbers, addresses)
- **Attribution:** ShinyHunters group
- **Details:** Luxury fashion house Chanel fell victim to a third-party Salesforce CRM compromise. Hackers (linked to ShinyHunters) used voice-phishing and OAuth token abuse to access Chanel's customer contact database, stealing U.S. client names, emails, phone numbers and addresses. No payment or password data was taken, but the incident raised phishing risks and reputational harm. This was part of the broader ShinyHunters campaign targeting Salesforce integrations across multiple companies globally.
- **Source:** [CM Alliance - August 2025](https://cm-alliance.com)

### Aug 5, 2025 – Pandora (Denmark)

- **Date:** August 5, 2025
- **Target:** Pandora (Danish jewelry giant)
- **Attack Type:** Data Breach via Salesforce attack
- **Impact:** Customer names, birthdates, and email addresses stolen
- **Attribution:** ShinyHunters campaign
- **Details:** Danish jewelry giant Pandora confirmed its customer data was stolen via a Salesforce attack tied to the ShinyHunters campaign. Names, birthdates, and email addresses of customers were exfiltrated, though financial, password, and ID information remained secure. The breach – part of a broader OAuth social-engineering wave – put thousands at risk of phishing and impersonation. Pandora notified affected customers and regulatory authorities in accordance with GDPR requirements.
- **Source:** [CM Alliance - August 2025](https://cm-alliance.com)

### Aug 6, 2025 – Air France–KLM (France/Netherlands)

- **Date:** August 6, 2025
- **Target:** Air France–KLM (International airline group)
- **Attack Type:** Data Breach via external customer-service platform
- **Impact:** Customer names, contact info, Flying Blue loyalty program data exposed
- **Attribution:** ShinyHunters/UNC groups (Salesforce-targeted attack)
- **Details:** The airline group disclosed unauthorized access to an external customer-service platform, resulting in exposure of customer names, contact info, Flying Blue loyalty program numbers/status, and email subject lines. No passwords, payment data or travel details were leaked. The incident was linked to a Salesforce-targeted attack likely by the ShinyHunters/UNC groups, prompting notifications to regulators and affected customers. This breach affected customers across multiple EU countries where Air France-KLM operates.
- **Source:** [CM Alliance - August 2025](https://cm-alliance.com)

### Aug 6, 2025 – Bouygues Telecom (France)

- **Date:** August 6, 2025
- **Target:** Bouygues Telecom (French telecom operator)
- **Attack Type:** Cyber Attack & Data Breach
- **Impact:** ~6.4 million customers' personal data stolen
- **Attribution:** Criminal group (specific attribution not disclosed)
- **Details:** French telecom operator Bouygues Telecom suffered a major intrusion by a criminal group, leading to the theft of personal data for ~6.4 million customers. Exposed information included names, contact details, customer IDs, contract details and IBAN bank numbers. The company contained the breach, notified France's CNIL regulator, and alerted customers, emphasizing that passwords and credit card data were not compromised. This was one of the largest telecom breaches in France in 2025, raising significant privacy concerns.
- **Source:** [CM Alliance - August 2025](https://cm-alliance.com)

### Aug 13, 2025 – Clinical Diagnostics Lab (Netherlands)

- **Date:** August 13, 2025
- **Target:** Clinical Diagnostics Lab in Rijswijk (Medical laboratory)
- **Attack Type:** Ransomware/Data Extortion
- **Impact:** ~485,000 Dutch women's cervical cancer screening records exposed
- **Attribution:** Nova ransomware group
- **Details:** A medical lab in Rijswijk was attacked by the Nova ransomware group, which stole ~300GB of sensitive data, including cervical cancer screening records of ~485,000 Dutch women. The attackers demanded a multimillion-euro ransom, ultimately forcing the lab to pay to prevent public release of patient data. About 100MB of data had been leaked as proof, and the incident raised serious privacy concerns in the EU health sector. Dutch data protection authorities launched an investigation into the lab's security measures and compliance with GDPR.
- **Source:** [CM Alliance - August 2025](https://cm-alliance.com)

### Aug 27, 2025 – Miljödata (Sweden)

- **Date:** August 27, 2025
- **Target:** Miljödata (Swedish IT service provider for municipalities)
- **Attack Type:** Ransomware Attack
- **Impact:** HR and sick-leave management systems disrupted across ~200 Swedish municipalities (~80% of Sweden's local authorities)
- **Attribution:** Not disclosed (investigation ongoing)
- **Details:** Miljödata, an IT service provider for municipal governments, was hit by ransomware, disrupting HR and sick-leave management systems across roughly 200 Swedish municipalities. With ~80% of Sweden's local authorities affected, employees were unable to register absences. The attack highlighted the risk of single points of failure in the public-sector supply chain. Swedish authorities emphasized the need for better supply chain security and redundancy in critical municipal IT services.
- **Source:** [CM Alliance - August 2025](https://cm-alliance.com)

### Late July/Early Aug 2025 – Dutch Caribbean Governments

- **Date:** Late July 2025 (reported early August)
- **Target:** Government bodies in Dutch Caribbean (Aruba and Curaçao)
- **Attack Type:** Coordinated cyberattacks
- **Impact:** Tax offices, court system, Aruba's Parliament - IT outages, halted tax collection and email services
- **Attribution:** Unknown (investigation ongoing)
- **Details:** Multiple government bodies in the Dutch Caribbean (Aruba and Curaçao, part of the Kingdom of the Netherlands) suffered coordinated cyberattacks that came to light in early August. Tax offices, a joint court system, and Aruba's Parliament saw IT outages that halted tax collection and email services. Dutch cybersecurity experts were dispatched to assist, and authorities issued alerts across the EU's overseas territories. While the perpetrators remained unknown, the incident highlighted the reach of cyber threats even in smaller EU-associated regions and prompted reviews of government network security.
- **Source:** [CM Alliance - August 2025](https://cm-alliance.com)

## Notable Attacks on EU Organizations

### Aug 28, 2025 – PayPal/European Banks: Systems Glitch

- **Date:** August 28, 2025
- **Target:** PayPal and European banking sector
- **Attack Type:** Non-malicious cybersecurity incident (systems glitch)
- **Impact:** Billions of euros in PayPal-linked direct debit transactions frozen
- **Attribution:** Not an attack - PayPal fraud prevention system malfunction
- **Details:** A non-malicious cybersecurity incident caused chaos in Europe's banking sector when PayPal's fraud prevention system malfunctioned. Several major German banks (e.g. BayernLB, Helaba, DZ Bank) detected false red flags and froze billions of euros in PayPal-linked direct debit transactions. For hours, legitimate payments were blocked and online merchants saw delays, until PayPal fixed the issue. The event, though not an attack, underscored the fragility of interconnected financial systems and the need for robust fail-safes against automated security errors.
- **Source:** [Cybersecurity Review - August 2025](https://cybersecurity-review.com)

### Ongoing – Russian Cyber Sabotage Threat

- **Date:** Throughout August 2025
- **Target:** European infrastructure and government networks
- **Attack Type:** State-sponsored cyber aggression and sabotage
- **Attribution:** Russian state-backed actors and pro-Russian groups
- **Details:** Throughout August, European officials warned of heightened cyber aggression linked to the Russia-Ukraine war. Norway's security service PST revealed that a pro-Russian group sabotaged a Norwegian dam (opening a floodgate) in April, and the UK continued accusing Russia of a "reckless" sabotage campaign targeting European infrastructure. EU entities and NATO allies ramped up vigilance around energy, transport, and communications networks as intelligence suggested Moscow-backed actors might seek to cause chaos and intimidate countries supporting Ukraine. This trend spurred regional cyber defense cooperation and infrastructure penetration testing across the EU.
- **Source:** [Reuters - Norway dam sabotage](https://reuters.com)

### Italian Hotels Data Breach

- **Date:** August 2025
- **Target:** Italian hotel chains
- **Attack Type:** Data breach
- **Impact:** ~90,000 passport IDs and personal information exposed
- **Attribution:** Not specified
- **Details:** Multiple Italian hotels suffered a data breach exposing approximately 90,000 passport IDs and personal information of guests. The incident highlighted vulnerabilities in the hospitality sector's handling of identity documents and customer data, raising concerns about compliance with GDPR data minimization requirements.
- **Source:** [CM Alliance - August 2025](https://cm-alliance.com)

### Orange Belgium

- **Date:** August 2025
- **Target:** Orange Belgium (Telecom operator)
- **Attack Type:** Data breach
- **Impact:** ~850,000 customer accounts compromised
- **Attribution:** Not specified
- **Details:** Major Belgian telecom firm Orange Belgium confirmed a cyber attack that compromised data on 850,000 customer accounts.
