# June 2025 - EU Major Incidents

## Major Data Breaches

### June 9, 2025 – Cartier (France)

- **Date:** June 9, 2025
- **Target:** Cartier (luxury jeweler, France)
- **Attack Type:** Data breach
- **Impact:** Limited customer personal information stolen
- **Attribution:** Scattered Spider cybercrime group suspected
- **Details:** Luxury jeweler Cartier disclosed that hackers had compromised its systems and stolen customer data, including limited personal information. The breach was part of a spate of attacks on fashion brands. Cartier reported that only a subset of client data was taken, and it initiated security upgrades and customer notifications. Specific numbers were not made public, but the incident prompted increased security measures across the luxury retail sector.
- **Source:** [CM Alliance - June 2025 Cyber Attacks](https://cm-alliance.com), BleepingComputer

### June 17, 2025 – Scania (Sweden)

- **Date:** June 17, 2025 (breach occurred late May)
- **Target:** Scania (truck manufacturer, Sweden)
- **Attack Type:** Data theft and extortion
- **Impact:** Thousands of insurance claim documents stolen
- **Attribution:** Individual hacker alias "Hensi"
- **Details:** Truck-maker Scania confirmed a breach of its insurance claim portal in late May, in which an extortionist stole thousands of claim documents using stolen third-party credentials. The hacker, using the alias "Hensi," attempted to sell the data on the dark web. Sensitive insurance claim information was exposed, though Scania indicated the overall fallout was limited. Scania refused to pay the ransom demand and involved law enforcement. The incident highlighted risks from compromised vendor credentials.
- **Source:** [CM Alliance - June 2025 Cyber Attacks](https://cm-alliance.com), BleepingComputer

### June 26, 2025 – Ahold Delhaize (Netherlands)

- **Date:** June 26, 2025 (disclosure date; attack occurred November 2024)
- **Target:** Ahold Delhaize (Dutch retail giant)
- **Attack Type:** Ransomware
- **Impact:** 2.2 million people's personal, financial, and health data exposed
- **Attribution:** INC Ransom group
- **Details:** Dutch-based retail giant Ahold Delhaize revealed that a November 2024 ransomware attack on its U.S. subsidiaries had exposed personal, financial, and health data of approximately 2.2 million people. The disclosure was made in June once the full impact was assessed. The "INC Ransom" group was believed responsible, having leaked samples of stolen files on its leak site. Compromised data included employee and customer records. The company enhanced security measures and coordinated with regulators following the breach.
- **Source:** [CM Alliance - June 2025 Cyber Attacks](https://cm-alliance.com), BleepingComputer

## Notable Attacks on EU Organizations

### Ransomware on Municipal Services

#### Glasgow City Council (UK)

- **Date:** June 2025
- **Target:** Glasgow City Council (Scotland, UK)
- **Attack Type:** Ransomware
- **Impact:** Public services offline, citizen services disrupted
- **Attribution:** Not disclosed; likely criminal ransomware gang
- **Details:** A ransomware attack struck Glasgow City Council in June, forcing public services offline. Hackers left ransom notes that disabled citizen services like payments and permitting, impacting thousands of residents. Payment and planning services were suspended, damaging public trust and slowing operations. The incident resulted in several days of service outages and costly recovery efforts, though no confirmation of data theft was made public. While Glasgow (in the UK) is not an EU member, this incident resonated across Europe as a warning to local governments about the vulnerability of under-resourced municipal IT systems.
- **Source:** [Strobes Security - Top 6 Data Breaches in June 2025](https://strobes.co), [SANS NewsBites](https://sans.org), The Scottish Sun

### Airline Disruption - WestJet

- **Date:** June 2025
- **Target:** WestJet (Canadian carrier with European operations)
- **Attack Type:** Malware attack on third-party IT provider
- **Impact:** Flights grounded, transatlantic routes disrupted
- **Details:** Canadian carrier WestJet suffered a cyber incident affecting European operations in June, coinciding with peak travel season. WestJet had to temporarily ground flights and shut down parts of its network, with ripple effects on transatlantic routes. The incident was reportedly due to a malware attack on a third-party IT provider, causing reservation and check-in systems to go down. Passengers in EU hubs faced delays, highlighting supply-chain vulnerabilities. WestJet worked with Canadian and EU cybersecurity teams to restore systems.

### Phishing Campaigns Targeting EU Agencies

- **Date:** June 2025
- **Target:** EU Member State ministries
- **Attack Type:** Phishing campaign
- **Attribution:** Likely state-backed group
- **Details:** A wave of phishing emails impersonating EU Commission officials was observed targeting multiple Member State ministries. No major breach of EU bodies was reported, but the European Commission's CERT issued advisories about these attempted intrusions. The campaign was attributed to a likely state-backed group and underscores an ongoing pattern of espionage-oriented attacks in Europe amid heightened geopolitical tensions. EU institutions remained on alert throughout the month.

## Other EU-Related Breaches

### Oxford City Council (UK)

While occurring outside EU jurisdiction, an IT services hack at Oxford City Council exposed two decades of staff data, affecting EU travelers and highlighting similar vulnerabilities across European local governments.

### British Airways Avios Partner

An attack on British Airways' Avios loyalty program partner affected EU travelers, though the incident was part of broader global targeting rather than EU-specific.
