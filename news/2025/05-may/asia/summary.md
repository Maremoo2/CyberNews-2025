# May 2025 - Asia Summary

## Overview

Asia's cybersecurity landscape in May 2025 was characterized by high-stakes state cyber operations, financially motivated attacks on institutions, and an ongoing effort to bolster cyber resilience across the region. The month saw significant geopolitical cyber tensions, sophisticated financial fraud, and continued targeting of critical sectors across the diverse Asian landscape.

## Key Trends

### State-Sponsored Cyber Operations

The interplay of state-sponsored attacks and regional conflicts dominated May's threat landscape:

- **China-Taiwan Tensions**: Cyber accusations escalated between China and Taiwan on May 27, with both sides publicly blaming each other for attacks on critical systems. This demonstrates that cyber warfare rhetoric is increasingly public and suggests a shadow war aiming to undermine government or military capabilities
- **India-Pakistan Cyber Skirmishes**: During brief military tensions in early May, hackers from both sides engaged in website defacements and data leaks, adding a cyber dimension to the regional conflict
- **Russian Operations in Central Asia**: The Operation Tajik campaign revealed Russian-aligned hackers targeting government and research entities in Tajikistan, demonstrating spillover into Central Asia

These incidents indicate that Asian nation-states (and their proxies) are very active in cyberspace, targeting political, military, and economic assets of rivals.

### Financial System Targeting

Asia's advanced financial markets proved to be high-value targets:

- **Japan's $2 Billion Trading Fraud**: The FSA reported nearly $2 billion in unauthorized stock trades executed through compromised online brokerage accounts, representing a unique form of cyber fraud that can have wider economic implications
- **Cryptocurrency Vulnerabilities**: Asian exchanges and DeFi platforms remained at significant risk, with earlier 2025 seeing Bybit's record $1.5 billion hack
- **Stock Market Manipulation**: Hackers exploiting trading accounts to conduct pump-and-dump schemes demonstrates sophisticated financial cybercrime

### Ransomware and Cybercrime Growth

While Asia saw fewer publicly reported ransomware incidents than Western regions, the threat was growing:

- Reports of ransomware attacks on Indian media conglomerate and Japanese steel manufacturer in Q2 2025
- Southeast Asian countries (Malaysia, Indonesia, Vietnam) experiencing increased activity from established groups like ALPHV
- Likely significant underreporting compared to Western countries
- Regional law enforcement cooperation improving through Interpol operations

### Insider Threats and Supply Chain Vulnerabilities

Several incidents highlighted internal security challenges:

- **Coupang Breach**: Former employee with retained access keys identified as prime suspect in massive breach
- **Star Health Insurance**: Hacker "Xenzen" hiring local individuals to deliver physical threats to executives, demonstrating how cyber threats can manifest in physical intimidation
- These cases stressed the need for robust insider risk programs and access control

### Rapid Digitization Challenges

The pandemic-era digital boom created new attack surfaces:

- **Mobile Banking Trojans**: Southeast Asia experienced a wave targeting popular financial apps
- **Super-App Phishing**: Scams targeting users of ride-hailing and payment platforms
- **Thailand and Philippines**: Identified as hotspots for malware distribution (per Trend Micro)
- **User Awareness Gap**: Many users still employing weak passwords or falling for basic social engineering

## Legislation & Policy Updates

### China

- Continued strict enforcement of Cybersecurity Law and Data Security Law
- **May 2025**: New guidelines for critical information infrastructure (CII) operators requiring enhanced data encryption and on-premises data storage
- Internal discussions about potential Personal Data Protection regulation similar to GDPR (no draft yet)
- National security focus evident in attributing attacks to foreign governments

### India

- **Digital Personal Data Protection Bill 2025**: In parliamentary committee review by May, proposing heavy fines for breaches and 72-hour reporting requirements
- **Cybersecurity Law Draft**: Ministry of Electronics and IT (MeitY) released strategy paper outlining need for overarching law to consolidate cyber incident reporting and critical infrastructure protection
- **VPN and Crypto Requirements**: CERT-In enforced 2022 logging directives for VPN providers and crypto exchanges in May

### Japan

- **National Cybersecurity Strategy Amendment**: Parliament deliberating changes to address supply chain security in finance sector
- **FSA Response to Trading Incident**: Urged all brokerages to implement hardware security keys for 2FA; hinted at regulatory action if not adopted
- **Cybersecurity Basic Act Review**: Potentially broadening authorities of National Center of Incident Readiness and Strategy for Cybersecurity (NISC)
- **U.S.-Japan Cyber Dialogue**: Late May focus on securing semiconductor supply chains

### South Korea

- **Personal Information Protection Commission (PIPC)**: New rules in May requiring breach notification within 24 hours (down from 72)
- **EU Alignment**: Working on law mirroring EU's NIS2 directive for operators of essential services (draft expected late 2025)
- **National Cyber Security Center**: Plans to establish center coordinating military and civilian response
- **EU-Korea Cyber Dialogue**: May 20 discussion on policy developments and cooperation

### Southeast Asia

- **Singapore**: Updated Cybersecurity Code of Practice for critical sectors in May, adding requirements for securing Industrial Control Systems (ICS)
- **Malaysia**: Finalizing Cybersecurity Bill to define roles and penalties (expected to be tabled in Parliament later in 2025)
- **Vietnam**: Implemented new directives under 2024 Law on Cybersecurity focusing on local data storage and content controls

## Statistics

### Volume of Attacks

- **Asia accounted for 25%** of the world's cyberattacks in the first part of 2025
- **India**: Faced ~265 million attacks in 2025 up to May (per Times of India report)
- **China**: Officials mentioned thwarting "millions" of foreign cyber intrusion attempts (precise numbers classified)
- **Southeast Asia**: ~400+ ransomware attacks across ASEAN countries in Q1-Q2 2025

### Data Breaches and Population Impact

- **India**: Over 50 million personal records breached in 2025 (bulk from Star Health and government ID leaks)
- **Japan**: Fewer incidents by number but high in sensitivity (brokerage hacks, ~10 million users in job search site breach)
- **China**: Hacker "GDPR" claimed 40 million Chinese citizens' data from various leaks (unverified)
- **South Korea**: 33.7 million users affected in Coupang breach (discovered later in 2025)

### Most Targeted Industries in Asia

1. **Government sector** (40% of targeted attack incidents - per Kaspersky Asia report)
2. **Finance and banking** (especially in Vietnam and Philippines with banking malware cases)
3. **Telecom and Media** (due to espionage and surveillance motives)
4. **Small and Medium Enterprises (SMEs)** (50% YoY increase in BEC in East Asia - per Trend Micro)

### Cryptocurrency-Related Crime

- **$1.2 billion in crypto stolen globally** through May 2025, with large portion involving Asia
- **Half from single exchange hack** linked to North Korea earlier in year
- **May incidents**: Cetus Protocol's $223M theft, ~$5M hack of Singapore-based exchange
- **North Korean hackers** (Lazarus Group) suspected in multiple DeFi platform attacks targeting Asia

### User Awareness and Response

- **Japan**: Over 70% of internet users aware of "ransomware" term (up from ~50% two years prior)
- **Indonesia**: 40% still use weak passwords or reuse them (correlating with high online fraud rates)
- **Singapore**: Phishing click-through rates in government cyber drills dropped to 5% in 2025 (from 8% in 2024)
- **General Trend**: Growing awareness in developed Asian economies, but basic cyber hygiene remains challenge in developing regions

## Impact Assessment

- **High geopolitical tension** manifesting in public cyber accusations between nations
- **$2 billion in unauthorized trades** in Japan highlighting financial system vulnerabilities
- **Millions of personal records** compromised across multiple breaches
- **Regional conflicts** increasingly featuring cyber dimensions
- **Growing insider threat** concerns across major tech companies
- **Rapid digital adoption** outpacing security awareness in some regions

## Looking Ahead

Asia's cyber landscape demonstrates a region under heavy cyber pressure but actively mobilizing defenses. While accounting for a large share of global cyber incidents (commensurate with its population and economic size), Asia's efforts in policy development, awareness campaigns, and regional cooperation are crucial to improving the security posture.

The statistics portray Asia as facing sophisticated threats - often state-linked and high-impact - despite relatively small numbers of breaches in some countries. The diversity of the region means cyber readiness varies widely, from highly advanced programs in Japan, South Korea, and Singapore to developing capabilities in Southeast Asian nations.

Key focus areas for the region include:
- Strengthening international cooperation on cybercrime (through organizations like Interpol)
- Harmonizing cybersecurity standards across borders
- Improving user education and awareness
- Addressing insider threats and supply chain vulnerabilities
- Balancing national security concerns with privacy rights in emerging data protection laws
- Securing critical financial infrastructure and cryptocurrency platforms

The overall trajectory shows that Asian nations are recognizing cybersecurity as integral to national security and economic stability, with significant policy developments expected to continue shaping the region's cyber landscape throughout 2025 and beyond.
