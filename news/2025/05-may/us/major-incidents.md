# May 2025 - US Major Incidents

## Major Data Breaches

### May 28, 2025 – LexisNexis Risk Solutions

- **Date:** May 28, 2025 (breach occurred December 2024, confirmed May 2025)
- **Target:** LexisNexis Risk Solutions (American data analytics firm)
- **Attack Type:** Data breach (third-party software exploit)
- **Impact:** Personal information of approximately 364,000 individuals compromised including names, addresses, and other sensitive records
- **Attribution:** Unknown cybercriminal group; under investigation by law enforcement
- **Details:** LexisNexis disclosed that attackers stole personal information of approximately 364,000 individuals in a breach that occurred in December 2024 but was only confirmed and disclosed in May 2025. The compromised data included names, addresses, and other sensitive records held by the legal and business intelligence provider. The investigation revealed the breach was enabled through exploitation of third-party software.
- **Source:** [BleepingComputer](https://bleepingcomputer.com), [CM Alliance](https://cm-alliance.com)

### May 1, 2025 – Ascension Health (St. Louis, MO)

- **Date:** May 1, 2025
- **Target:** Ascension Health (one of the largest U.S. healthcare systems)
- **Attack Type:** Data breach (vendor vulnerability)
- **Impact:** 430,000 patients' medical and personal data compromised
- **Attribution:** Unspecified criminal hacking group; no ransomware deployed (data theft for likely sale)
- **Details:** Ascension announced that 430,000 patients' medical and personal data were compromised in what was Ascension's second major cyber incident within a year. Attackers exploited a vulnerability in a former third-party billing partner's software to exfiltrate extensive patient records including names, diagnoses, and contact information. The breach represented one of the largest healthcare data exposures in May.
- **Source:** [The Register](https://theregister.com), [CM Alliance](https://cm-alliance.com)

### May 14, 2025 – Coinbase

- **Date:** May 14, 2025 (extortion demand sent May 11)
- **Target:** Coinbase (cryptocurrency exchange)
- **Attack Type:** Data extortion attempt (insider-assisted breach)
- **Impact:** Less than 1% of customers affected; names and contact information stolen through insider scheme
- **Attribution:** Unknown threat actor group operating via recruited insiders; believed to be financially motivated extortionists
- **Details:** Coinbase revealed an insider scheme that led to theft of customer data. Hackers recruited support agents abroad and obtained a list of Coinbase users' names and contact information (less than 1% of customers). On May 11, the attackers emailed Coinbase demanding $20 million or they would release the stolen data. Coinbase refused to pay and instead offered a $20 million bounty to identify the perpetrators. The incident highlighted the growing threat of insider-assisted breaches in the cryptocurrency sector.
- **Source:** [Coinbase SEC filing and blog](https://coinbase.com), [The Record](https://therecord.media), [CM Alliance](https://cm-alliance.com)

### May 7, 2025 – Insight Partners

- **Date:** May 7, 2025 (breach occurred January 2025, disclosed May)
- **Target:** Insight Partners (New York-based venture capital firm)
- **Attack Type:** Data breach (private equity)
- **Impact:** Sensitive data of employees and investors (limited partners) stolen
- **Attribution:** Not disclosed publicly; possibly state-sponsored espionage angle due to high-profile nature of victims
- **Details:** Insight Partners confirmed that sensitive data of employees and investors (limited partners) was stolen in a cyberattack. The breach actually occurred in January 2025 but was disclosed in May after a thorough investigation. Compromised data included personal and financial information on individuals connected to the firm. Given the high-profile nature of the victims, some security analysts speculated about potential state-sponsored espionage motives.
- **Source:** [BleepingComputer](https://bleepingcomputer.com), [CM Alliance](https://cm-alliance.com)

### May 1, 2025 – Barnstable County Sheriff's Office (Massachusetts)

- **Date:** May 1, 2025
- **Target:** Barnstable County Sheriff's Office (Massachusetts)
- **Attack Type:** Insider data leak
- **Impact:** Personal information of over 100 current and former employees leaked
- **Attribution:** Insider (a staff member placed on leave pending investigation)
- **Details:** Personal information of over 100 current and former employees was leaked from the sheriff's office database. A sheriff's office employee is suspected of unlawfully accessing and sharing data including names, addresses, and Social Security numbers. No external hackers were involved, but the incident highlighted critical internal security lapses in law enforcement organizations.
- **Source:** [CapeNews](https://capenews.net), [CM Alliance](https://cm-alliance.com)

### May 2025 (various) – Higher Education Breaches

- **Date:** May 2025 (multiple incidents)
- **Target:** Multiple U.S. universities and school districts
- **Attack Type:** Hacking and data theft via education software provider
- **Impact:** Tens of thousands of students and staff affected across multiple states
- **Attribution:** PowerSchool-related attacks linked to unidentified hacker who first breached the ed-tech vendor in 2024; other university breaches claimed by ransomware gangs like Clop
- **Details:** Multiple U.S. universities and school districts reported breaches in May. Notably, hackers accessed data from PowerSchool, an education software provider, and then extorted individual school districts by threatening to leak student data. The attacker leveraged data stolen from the vendor in 2024 to conduct ongoing extortion campaigns against schools. Separately, some university systems experienced breaches (e.g., ongoing fallout at Western New Mexico University from an April attack). Collectively, these incidents affected tens of thousands of students and staff across multiple states, reflecting a disturbing trend of attacks targeting the education sector.
- **Source:** [CM Alliance](https://cm-alliance.com)

## Notable Security Incidents & Attacks

### May 20, 2025 – Kettering Health (Ohio)

- **Date:** May 20, 2025
- **Target:** Kettering Health (network of 14 hospitals and 120+ clinics in Ohio)
- **Attack Type:** Ransomware attack
- **Impact:** System-wide IT outage forcing cancellation of elective surgeries and appointments; emergency rooms operated on backup processes
- **Attribution:** Interlock ransomware gang (also tracked as "Nefarious Mantis")
- **Details:** A system-wide IT outage struck Kettering Health, forcing the cancellation of elective surgeries and appointments across their network of 14 hospitals and 120+ outpatient facilities. Emergency rooms stayed open using backup paper-based processes. The outage was confirmed to be caused by a cyberattack, with cybersecurity firms later tying it to the emerging Interlock ransomware operation. The attackers threatened to leak stolen medical data if ransom was not paid. This incident represented one of the most disruptive healthcare ransomware attacks of May.
- **Source:** [BleepingComputer](https://bleepingcomputer.com), CNN

### May 19, 2025 – Nucor Corporation (North Carolina)

- **Date:** May 19, 2025
- **Target:** Nucor Corporation (largest U.S. steelmaker)
- **Attack Type:** Cyberattack on manufacturing/steel production
- **Impact:** Production outage at some steel mills; delayed steel shipments
- **Attribution:** Not publicly disclosed; likely ransomware or wiper attack based on disruptive nature
- **Details:** Nucor, the largest U.S. steelmaker, suffered a production outage after a "cybersecurity incident" forced some steel mills offline. The company proactively shut down systems at certain facilities to contain the attack, resulting in delayed steel shipments to customers. While Nucor did not confirm ransomware, the disruptive nature of the incident suggests a likely ransomware or wiper attack. The incident underscored significant risks to critical manufacturing infrastructure.
- **Source:** [The Record](https://therecord.media), [CM Alliance](https://cm-alliance.com)

### May 12, 2025 – State of Alabama

- **Date:** May 12, 2025
- **Target:** Alabama state government
- **Attack Type:** Government IT outage (suspected malware via phishing)
- **Impact:** Disruption of state services and websites; some public-facing portals intermittently offline
- **Attribution:** Unknown attackers; investigation suggested malware introduced via phishing email
- **Details:** The Alabama state government warned of a "cybersecurity event" that disrupted state services and websites. Some public-facing portals were intermittently offline for several days. Officials did not initially specify the cause, but the widespread disruption raised concerns of a possible ransomware attack on state networks. State IT investigation suggested the incident may have been caused by malware introduced via a phishing email. Services were restored after several days of remediation efforts.
- **Source:** [The Record](https://therecord.media), [CM Alliance](https://cm-alliance.com)

### May 27, 2025 – Sheboygan County, Wisconsin

- **Date:** May 27, 2025 (attack occurred late April, disclosed May)
- **Target:** Sheboygan County, Wisconsin
- **Attack Type:** Ransomware (data breach)
- **Impact:** Unauthorized access to personal data of approximately 67,000 residents including Social Security numbers and other PII
- **Attribution:** Chort ransomware gang
- **Details:** The county disclosed that a ransomware attack in late April resulted in unauthorized access to personal data of about 67,000 residents. The breach included Social Security numbers and other personally identifiable information from county systems. Notifications were sent out to affected residents in May. The Chort ransomware gang claimed responsibility for the attack. This incident, though affecting a local government, was notable for the large number of individuals impacted relative to the community size.
- **Source:** [The Record](https://therecord.media), [CM Alliance](https://cm-alliance.com)

### May 14, 2025 – Cellcom (Wisconsin telecom)

- **Date:** May 14-20, 2025
- **Target:** Cellcom (regional mobile carrier, Wisconsin and Upper Michigan)
- **Attack Type:** Cyberattack causing service outage
- **Impact:** Multi-day outage of voice and SMS services; many subscribers couldn't make calls or send texts for nearly a week
- **Attribution:** Not disclosed; possibly ransomware or DDoS
- **Details:** Regional mobile carrier Cellcom experienced a multi-day outage of voice and SMS services for customers across Wisconsin and Upper Michigan. On May 20, Cellcom confirmed the disruption was due to a cyberattack on its network infrastructure. Many subscribers couldn't make calls or send texts for nearly a week, representing a significant service impact. The prolonged nature of the service disruption pointed to a serious network compromise, though the specific attack type (ransomware or DDoS) was not publicly disclosed.
- **Source:** [BleepingComputer](https://bleepingcomputer.com), [CM Alliance](https://cm-alliance.com)

### May 29, 2025 – Covenant Health (Maine/New Hampshire)

- **Date:** May 27-29, 2025 (ongoing into early June)
- **Target:** Covenant Health Catholic healthcare system (Maine and New Hampshire)
- **Attack Type:** Ransomware attack
- **Impact:** Three hospitals affected (St. Joseph's and St. Mary's in Maine, St. Joseph's in Nashua, NH); all IT systems taken offline; staff reverted to paper records; limited services
- **Attribution:** Suspected ransomware (group not publicly named as negotiations ongoing)
- **Details:** Three hospitals in Maine and New Hampshire (St. Joseph's and St. Mary's in Maine, plus St. Joseph's in Nashua, NH) were hit by a cyberattack on their parent Catholic healthcare system. All IT systems were preemptively taken offline to contain the threat, forcing staff to revert to paper records and significantly limiting services. The attack occurred May 27-28, with ongoing disruptions extending into early June. While ransomware was suspected, the specific group wasn't publicly named as negotiations were reportedly ongoing. This incident was part of a broader pattern of ransomware targeting U.S. healthcare infrastructure.
- **Source:** [The Record](https://therecord.media), [CM Alliance](https://cm-alliance.com)
