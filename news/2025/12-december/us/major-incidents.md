# December 2025 - US Major Incidents

## Major Data Breaches

### Aug 14–Dec 4, 2025 – Marquis Software Solutions (Fintech/MSP)

- **Date:** August 14 - December 4, 2025 (breach period), December 2025 (notification)
- **Target:** Marquis Software Solutions (Fintech/Managed Service Provider)
- **Attack Type:** Ransomware via SonicWall VPN exploit
- **Impact:** ~780,000 individuals' personal data potentially exposed; U.S. banks' customer data compromised
- **Attribution:** Ransomware group (specific attribution not disclosed)

#### Incident Details

**Attack Vector:**
- Exploitation of SonicWall VPN vulnerability
- Unauthorized access to network infrastructure
- Systems storing U.S. banks' customer data compromised
- Prolonged access period (August to December)

**Affected Organization Profile:**
- Marquis Software Solutions: Fintech company and managed service provider
- Serves multiple U.S. financial institutions
- Processes and stores sensitive banking customer data
- Critical service provider in financial services supply chain

**Exposed Data:**
- Names and addresses
- Dates of birth
- Social Security numbers (SSNs)
- Financial account information
- Phone numbers
- Approximately 780,000 individuals affected

**Response and Notification:**
- Investigation ongoing throughout December
- Banks notifying affected customers
- Credit monitoring services offered to victims
- Enhanced security measures implemented
- Federal financial regulators notified

**Significance:**
- Supply chain attack on financial services sector
- MSP compromise affects multiple downstream clients
- SonicWall VPN vulnerabilities continue to be exploited
- Extended dwell time allowed comprehensive data exfiltration
- Highlights third-party risk in financial services

**Sources:** [Reuters - Fintech firm Marquis notifies affected business after ransomware breach](https://reuters.com)

### Nov 18, 2025 (Disclosed Dec 23) – University of Phoenix (Arizona)

- **Date:** November 18, 2025 (breach), December 23, 2025 (disclosure)
- **Target:** University of Phoenix (For-profit educational institution)
- **Attack Type:** CL0P ransomware; Oracle E-Business Suite zero-day exploitation (CVE-2025-61882)
- **Impact:** Approximately 3.5 million student, staff, and supplier records stolen
- **Attribution:** CL0P ransomware gang

#### Incident Details

**Attack Methodology:**
- Exploitation of zero-day vulnerability in Oracle E-Business Suite (CVE-2025-61882)
- CL0P ransomware gang gained access to university network
- Data exfiltration before encryption deployment
- Sophisticated attack targeting enterprise resource planning system

**Affected Population:**
- 3,489,274 individuals impacted (per notification to Maine Attorney General)
- Current and former students
- Alumni records
- University staff and faculty
- Suppliers and business partners

**Stolen Data:**
- Names and addresses
- Social Security numbers (SSNs)
- Dates of birth
- Financial records (tuition payments, financial aid)
- Academic records
- Contact information
- Employment information for staff

**Timeline:**
- November 18, 2025: Breach occurs
- Investigation period: Late November - mid-December
- December 23, 2025: Public disclosure and notification letters sent
- Notifications filed with Maine Attorney General's office

**CL0P Gang Profile:**
- Prolific ransomware and extortion group
- Known for exploiting zero-day vulnerabilities
- Previous campaigns: MOVEit Transfer (2023), GoAnywhere (2023)
- Trend toward targeting enterprise software vulnerabilities
- Data theft and extortion rather than encryption focus

**University Response:**
- Immediate investigation launched
- Forensic cybersecurity firm engaged
- Law enforcement notified
- Notification to all affected individuals
- Enhanced security posture implementation
- No indication ransom was paid

**Implications:**
- Educational sector continues to be major target
- Zero-day vulnerabilities in enterprise software critical risk
- Large alumni databases attractive to threat actors
- Financial aid information particularly sensitive
- Student loan servicer integration creates additional risk

**Sources:** [BleepingComputer - University of Phoenix data breach impacts nearly 3.5 million individuals](https://bleepingcomputer.com)

## Law Enforcement Actions

### Dec 18, 2025 – E-Note Cryptocurrency Exchange Seizure

- **Date:** December 18, 2025
- **Target:** E-Note cryptocurrency exchange
- **Action Type:** Law enforcement takedown and seizure
- **Impact:** ~$70 million seized; major ransomware money laundering platform dismantled
- **Agencies:** U.S. DOJ, FBI, Michigan State Police, Finnish National Bureau of Investigation, German Police

#### Operation Details

**International Coordination:**
- U.S. Department of Justice led operation
- Federal Bureau of Investigation
- Michigan State Police
- Finnish National Bureau of Investigation
- German Police
- Coordinated multi-country law enforcement action

**Assets Seized:**
- Domains: e-note.com, e-note.ws, jabb.mn
- Mobile applications associated with exchange
- Servers and infrastructure
- Cryptocurrency wallets
- Approximately $70 million in proceeds

**Criminal Activity:**
- Platform used extensively by ransomware gangs
- Money laundering for cybercriminal proceeds
- Facilitated conversion of illicit cryptocurrency
- Enabled cash-out for ransomware operators
- Provided anonymization services for criminals

**Operators:**
- Exchange operators arrested in coordinated action
- Multiple jurisdictions involved in arrests
- Criminal charges filed
- Extradition proceedings where applicable

**Asset Disposition:**
- Seized funds earmarked for victim compensation
- Asset forfeiture proceedings initiated
- Potential distribution to ransomware victims
- International cooperation on fund distribution

**Significance:**
- Disrupts critical infrastructure for ransomware economy
- Demonstrates effective international law enforcement cooperation
- Financial impact on ransomware operations
- Sends strong message to cryptocurrency exchanges facilitating crime
- Part of broader strategy to target ransomware financial networks

**Sources:** [BleepingComputer - US seizes E-Note crypto exchange for laundering ransomware payments](https://bleepingcomputer.com)

### Dec 18, 2025 – Tren de Aragua Gang (Venezuela/US)

- **Date:** December 18, 2025 (indictment)
- **Target:** Tren de Aragua transnational criminal organization
- **Action Type:** Federal criminal indictment
- **Impact:** 54 members charged; ATM jackpotting operations across multiple U.S. states
- **Agency:** U.S. Department of Justice, District of Nebraska

#### Indictment Details

**Organization Profile:**
- Tren de Aragua: Venezuelan transnational criminal gang
- Operations in Venezuela, South America, and United States
- Designated transnational criminal enterprise with terrorist ties
- Multi-faceted criminal activities including cyber-enabled crime

**Charges:**
- 54 members and leaders indicted
- Federal charges in U.S. District Court, Nebraska
- Multiple counts of conspiracy, fraud, and money laundering
- Criminal enterprise/RICO-related charges

**ATM Jackpotting Scheme:**
- Used malware to "jackpot" ATMs
- Forced cash dispensing without authorization
- Operations across multiple U.S. states
- Millions of dollars stolen from financial institutions
- Sophisticated technical capabilities

**Technical Methodology:**
- Malware deployment on ATM systems
- Physical and cyber access to machines
- Network exploitation to control cash dispensing
- Coordination across geographic locations
- Money mule networks for fund distribution

**DOJ Actions:**
- Labeled gang as transnational criminal organization
- Connected to terrorist activities
- Seized assets and funds
- Arrest warrants issued for fugitives
- International cooperation for extraditions

**Broader Criminal Activities:**
- Beyond ATM jackpotting: drug trafficking, human trafficking, extortion
- Cybercrime as funding mechanism for organization
- Technical sophistication increasing over time
- Expansion into U.S. territory strategic goal

**Significance:**
- Demonstrates convergence of traditional organized crime and cybercrime
- ATM jackpotting represents physical/cyber hybrid attack
- Transnational gangs increasingly using cyber capabilities
- Law enforcement adapting to organized crime cyber operations
- Financial institution security implications

**Sources:** [U.S. Department of Justice - Tren De Aragua Members and Leaders Indicted](https://justice.gov)

## Additional Security Incidents

### Sept 2025 (Disclosed Dec) – Atlanta Medical Center (Georgia)

- **Date:** September 2025 (attack), December 2025 (reports surfaced)
- **Target:** Atlanta Medical Center (Georgia healthcare facility)
- **Attack Type:** Ransomware (CL0P suspected)
- **Impact:** Hospital IT systems briefly shut down; patient care not compromised
- **Attribution:** CL0P ransomware gang (suspected, not confirmed)

#### Incident Details

**Limited Public Information:**
- Details sparse due to healthcare privacy and security concerns
- Reports emerged in December of August/September incident
- Hospital systems affected but limited operational impact
- Patient care continued without major disruption

**Response:**
- IT systems temporarily taken offline as precaution
- Emergency backup procedures activated
- Patient care protocols maintained
- Investigation by healthcare IT security specialists

**Healthcare Sector Context:**
- Healthcare continues to be major ransomware target
- Electronic health records valuable to attackers
- Patient safety considerations complicate response
- Regulatory requirements (HIPAA) affect disclosure

**Source:** Industry reports and security researchers

## FBI Prince Group Network Disruption

### Dec 2025 – FBI Prince Group Takedown

- **Date:** December 2025
- **Target:** Prince Group cryptocurrency money laundering network
- **Action Type:** FBI-led disruption operation
- **Impact:** ~$15 billion in illicit cryptocurrency confiscated
- **Agency:** Federal Bureau of Investigation

#### Operation Details

**Scope:**
- Massive FBI operation targeting international network
- Approximately $15 billion seized (one of largest ever)
- Network provided money laundering services to multiple criminal organizations
- International coordination for asset seizure

**Criminal Network:**
- Prince Group: Major cryptocurrency laundering operation
- Serviced ransomware gangs, fraud operators, drug traffickers
- Provided cryptocurrency tumbling and mixing services
- Facilitated conversion to traditional currency
- Global operations with nodes in multiple countries

**Law Enforcement Actions:**
- Cryptocurrency wallets seized
- Infrastructure dismantled
- Key operators arrested or identified
- Asset forfeiture proceedings
- Ongoing investigations into network clients

**Significance:**
- Largest or near-largest cryptocurrency seizure in history
- Disrupts major component of cybercrime financial infrastructure
- Sends message about capability to trace cryptocurrency
- Impact on ransomware economy significant
- Demonstrates advanced blockchain analysis capabilities

**Source:** [Help Net Security - Global law enforcement actions put pressure on cybercrime networks](https://helpnetsecurity.com)
