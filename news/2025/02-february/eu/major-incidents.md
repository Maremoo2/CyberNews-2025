# February 2025 - EU Major Incidents

## Major Data Breaches

### Feb 2 – Casio UK
- **Date:** February 2, 2025
- **Target:** Casio UK (Online Shop)
- **Attack Type:** Website compromise (Magecart)
- **Impact:** Credit card and personal data of customers stolen from the Casio.co.uk online shop
- **Attribution:** Unknown attackers injected malicious scripts into the checkout page
- **Details:** Shoppers who made purchases between Jan 14–24, 2025 had their payment details skimmed

### Feb 16 – Finastra (Fintech Provider)
- **Date:** February 16, 2025
- **Target:** Finastra (Global Fintech Company)
- **Attack Type:** Data breach (file server hack)
- **Impact:** Sensitive data (up to 400 GB) from one of the world's largest fintech companies was exfiltrated
- **Attribution:** Unknown, though a BreachForums actor "abyss0" claimed to be selling stolen data
- **Details:** Finastra disclosed that attackers had breached a secure file transfer system in Oct 2024 and accessed personal data, with victims notified in February

## Notable Attacks on EU Organizations

### Feb 25 – Orange S.A.
- **Date:** February 25, 2025
- **Target:** Orange Group (Telecom Provider)
- **Attack Type:** Hack-and-leak extortion
- **Impact:** Internal documents (source code, invoices, user records, employee data) stolen and leaked from Orange Group
- **Attribution:** A hacker using alias "Rey" (affiliated with HellCat ransomware group)
- **Details:** The attacker breached Orange's systems (reportedly in Orange's Romanian subsidiary) and, after an unsuccessful extortion attempt, leaked ~6.5 GB of data online. Analysis suggests much of the leaked data was outdated, though some sensitive info (customer and partner details) was exposed

### Feb 25 – Bulgarian Supreme Administrative Court
- **Date:** February 25, 2025
- **Target:** Bulgaria's Supreme Administrative Court
- **Attack Type:** Ransomware & data leak
- **Impact:** 3.5 TB of legal documents and personal data from Bulgaria's highest administrative court exfiltrated
- **Attribution:** Unknown (criminal group)
- **Details:** The breach targeted a Parliamentary committee's IT systems and exposed sensitive information on judges and court staff. The court confirmed its network was encrypted by ransomware and is investigating if the stolen data (which includes PII and HR files) was posted online

### Feb 10 – Port of Ostend (Belgium)
- **Date:** February 10, 2025
- **Target:** Port of Ostend (Belgian Critical Infrastructure)
- **Attack Type:** Cyberattack on critical infrastructure
- **Impact:** Disruption of port IT systems ("Ensor" ship movement and crew logging system offline)
- **Attribution:** Unknown
- **Details:** The Belgian Port of Ostend announced on Feb 12 that a cyberattack had hit its systems on Feb 10, prompting a federal cyber emergency response. While no data breach was reported, the incident raised concerns about maritime operational tech security
